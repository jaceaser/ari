## The Situation

The decedent died intestate — no will. The estate was modest: total value under $75,000. The only real property was the family home, a residence that qualified as a homestead under Texas law. The surviving heirs were the decedent's spouse and minor children.

In most states, an estate of this type would require at least some form of formal probate proceeding. In Texas, the Small Estate Affidavit procedure provides an alternative for qualifying estates that avoids the cost and time of a full administration.

## What the Operator Did

Texas Estates Code Chapter 205 establishes the Small Estate Affidavit process. The requirements: the estate must be worth $75,000 or less (excluding homestead and exempt property), no administration has been opened, the decedent died intestate, and at least 30 days have elapsed since the death.

The real property limitation is specific: under the SEA procedure, homestead property can pass to the surviving spouse and minor children as homestead heirs without going through full probate — but only when it's the homestead passing to those specific heirs. Other real property doesn't qualify for the SEA.

Counsel prepared the Small Estate Affidavit with the required supporting documentation: identification of all heirs, inventory of estate assets, confirmation that debts don't exceed non-exempt assets, and affidavits from the heirs themselves. The SEA was filed in the county court and received judicial approval within 30 days.

With the approved SEA recorded, the homestead vested in the surviving spouse and the minor children by operation of law — no executor, no administrator, no additional probate process.

## The Outcome

The investor acquired from the survivors at fair pricing — not a distressed discount in the traditional fractured equity sense, but a transaction that resolved cleanly and quickly. No formal probate was required. The survivors received consideration, the investor received clear title, and the transaction closed without the months-long delay of an administration.

## The Lesson

The Small Estate Affidavit is dramatically underutilized by practitioners outside of probate-focused work — and by investors who assume that any estate requires full probate. For qualifying estates, the SEA is faster, cheaper, and procedurally simpler than any alternative.

Know the qualifications precisely:
- Estate value under $75,000 (excluding homestead and exempt property)
- Decedent died intestate
- No administration opened or pending
- At least 30 days since death

The real property limitation matters. The SEA works for homestead passing to the surviving spouse and children — it doesn't open the door for all real property owned by the decedent. If the decedent owned investment property separate from the homestead, a different procedure will be required for that asset.

When the qualifications are met, reach for the SEA first. It resolves the estate quickly and gives the survivors — your future sellers — clear title to transfer.
