## The Situation

Ramon Delacruz died in El Paso with no will, no spouse, and one adult son named Javier. The house was worth $145,000. There were $9,000 in delinquent taxes owed against the property. Javier showed up holding nothing but a birth certificate. No probate had ever been opened, and the family had no intention of going through the court system.

The question on the table was simple: does Javier actually own this property, and if so, how do you document it and move it?

## What the Operator Did

Under Texas Estates Code § 201.001, when a decedent dies intestate with no surviving spouse and one child, that child takes 100% by operation of law at the moment of death. No probate is required to vest the title. Probate is one way to document the transfer — it is not the only way. The Affidavit of Heirship documents what already legally occurred.

The operator executed a Monster AOH with Javier named as the applicant. Two disinterested witnesses — a neighbor and a cousin who had known Ramon for years — swore to Ramon's full marital history and his sole-child status. The witnesses' affidavit established the chain: Ramon died, no surviving spouse, one child Javier, therefore Javier owns 100%.

After recording the AOH, the operator executed an Heir-to-Investor Deed from Javier conveying the property to the investor. The $9,000 in delinquent taxes were paid after recording to clear the tax obligation.

## The Outcome

The operator acquired 100% of the property for $22,500. The title commitment came back clean. The property resold 71 days later at $151,000. Net profit: $108,000 — on a transaction that a traditional buyer would have said needed probate first and could not close until that process completed.

## The Lesson

Single-heir intestate cases are the cleanest deals in the business. The AOH is the conveyance mechanism — probate is not required when one person inherits 100% by law. The title vests at the moment of death under Texas Estates Code § 201.001. The AOH documents what already happened. Most investors walk away from these deals because they see "no probate" and assume the title is broken. The title is not broken. It transferred the moment Ramon died. All Javier needed was the right document to prove it.
