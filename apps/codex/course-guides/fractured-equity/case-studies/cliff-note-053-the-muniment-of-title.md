## The Situation

The decedent had left a valid will, and at some point — either recently or in connection with the investor's outreach — the family had the will admitted to probate as muniment of title only. No executor was appointed. No administration was opened. No court supervision of estate management would occur.

Under Texas Estates Code § 257.001, muniment of title is available when the estate has no debts (except debts secured by real estate) and no administration is needed. The will is simply recorded as a court-established document proving who the heirs are and what they inherit. It's probate stripped to its essential function: establishing a clear chain from the decedent to the successors.

## What the Operator Did

When the investor encountered the muniment of title, the analysis was simple: the work is already done.

A muniment of title on record is functionally equivalent to a full Affidavit of Heirship from a title chain perspective — it tells the world who inherited and under what authority. The designated heirs' identities and shares were already established by court order. No additional heirship investigation was needed. No witnesses were required for a separate AOH.

The investor executed a Heir-to-Investor Deed directly from the muniment-designated heirs. The deed referenced the muniment of title in the recitals, establishing the chain: decedent → will → muniment → heirs → investor.

## The Outcome

Clean transaction at $32,000. The title commitment accepted the muniment of title as a valid foundation for title insurance without requiring additional documentation. No title exceptions related to heirship or probate were necessary beyond the standard underwriter requirements.

## The Lesson

Muniment of title is the simplest form of Texas probate, and it's exactly right for property acquisition. When you encounter a muniment-of-title estate, recognize what you have: the chain is already established by court order. Proceed directly to the Heir-to-Investor Deed.

The only thing to verify: that the will was admitted without objection, that no debts remain that could affect title, and that all designated heirs under the muniment are available to sign. If all heirs are present and cooperative, a muniment-of-title acquisition is often the fastest and cleanest fractured equity transaction possible.

Don't reinvent documentation that already exists. When the work is done, execute the deed and close.
