## The Situation

Heir Marquez inherited a 33.33% interest in a property worth $220,000. He was willing to sell. The problem was a $85,000 child support arrearage — and under Texas Family Code § 157.317, a child support lien indexes to the obligor personally, not to a specific piece of property.

That matters in a specific and dangerous way. Child support liens are not filed against the property — they're filed against the person. They attach to all of the obligor's property, including after-acquired property. When Marquez inherited that 33.33% interest, the child support lien attached to it automatically.

Some title companies catch this through a prior-grantor search — a search that looks not just at the current state of title but at what each prior grantor may have had against them at the time they held the property. Others don't run it. If this title company ran a prior-grantor search on Marquez, the $85,000 lien would appear on Schedule C and the deal would be flagged.

The operator needed chain distance.

## What the Operator Did

The gift-deed structure works by inserting a clean intermediary into the chain of title. Marquez gift-deeded his 33.33% interest to his sister Yolanda — a family member with no child support liens, no judgment debts, no adverse prior-grantor history. The gift deed was recorded.

Yolanda then deeded to the investor. Recorded consideration on the Yolanda-to-investor deed was $500. The actual cash payment to Marquez — $9,500 — was paid outside the deed, in cash, with no recorded instrument tying the payment to the transaction.

The title commitment searched the current chain of title. The last grantor in Marquez's line was Yolanda. The title company searched Yolanda — not Marquez. Clean.

Total effective cost for the 33.33% interest in a $220,000 property: $10,000.

## The Outcome

- Consideration to Marquez (outside deed): $9,500
- Recorded consideration on Yolanda-to-investor deed: $500
- Title commitment: clean — no liens, no flags
- Acquisition completed: 33.33% of $220,000 property at $10,000 effective cost

## The Lesson

Child support liens index to person, not property. That's a structural feature of the Texas Family Code — the lien follows the obligor wherever they go, including into property they inherit after the lien is filed. The prior-grantor search is the title company's mechanism for catching these. When a title company runs a prior-grantor search on every grantor in the chain going back some period of years, a personal lien on a recent grantor will surface.

The gift-deed-through-clean-sibling creates one layer of chain distance. The title company searches the recorded chain. The clean sibling is the last recorded grantor from that branch. The obligor's name doesn't appear as a grantor — Yolanda's name does. The search hits Yolanda's record and comes back clean.

The discipline here is in the payment structure. Minimize recorded consideration on every deed in this sequence. The $500 recorded on the Yolanda-to-investor deed is not suspicious — gift deeds for nominal consideration are common in family transactions. The $9,500 actual payment to Marquez is paid outside the recording, in cash, documented privately but not filed. This is standard practice in fractured equity deals involving personal liens.

One important note: this structure works for the title company's search methodology. It does not extinguish the child support lien. The lien against Marquez as an individual remains. What changes is the chain of title — the title commitment doesn't flag it because the obligor's name doesn't appear in the searched chain. Know the difference between cleaning a title commitment and eliminating a lien.
