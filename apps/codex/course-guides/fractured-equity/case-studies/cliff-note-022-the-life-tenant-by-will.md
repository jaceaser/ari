## The Situation

The decedent left a will that granted a life estate to the surviving spouse, with the remainder interest passing to the adult children upon her death. The surviving spouse is 71 years old, lives in the property, maintains it, and has no interest in selling. The adult children are cooperative and willing to sell their remainder interests. But the life tenant is the challenge — and without her, the investor cannot get possession.

Under Texas Estates Code, a life estate created by a will is an enforceable property interest. The life tenant has the right of possession for the duration of her natural life. She has the duty not to commit waste and the obligation to handle ordinary upkeep and taxes — but she has the right to be there. No foreclosure, no partition, no contribution claim removes a valid life tenant from property where she has a testamentary right to remain. The only path to possession is negotiation.

Codex Template 23, the Life Estate Release Deed, is the transaction document that converts this situation into a clean fee simple title.

## What the Operator Did

The operator began by acquiring the remainder interests from the adult children at prices reflecting the children's subordinate position — they hold the future interest, not current possession, and the property could be tied up in the life tenant's occupancy for decades. Total acquisition cost for all remainder interests: $22,000.

With 100% of the remainder interest in hand, the operator turned to the life tenant. The approach was professional and methodical. An actuarial valuation was performed using IRS § 7520 tables — the standard tool for valuing life estates. The inputs: age 71, property value $235,000, IRS life expectancy of 18 years for a 71-year-old. The actuarial value of her life estate came to $58,000.

The operator offered $42,000 plus moving assistance for her execution of the Life Estate Release Deed. The offer was below actuarial value — $16,000 below — but represented a real, immediate cash payment versus a theoretical future interest in a property she would need to maintain and insure. The investor also paid for the life tenant's independent legal counsel, which is not just courteous but operationally essential: a life estate release executed without independent counsel is a transaction with enforceability risk.

The life tenant accepted. She moved to her adult child's home.

## The Outcome

Total consolidation cost: $68,000. That covers $22,000 for all remainder interests from the children plus $42,000 for the life estate release plus moving assistance. The property sold for $241,000.

## The Lesson

Will-created life estates are not obstacles you route around — they are property interests you negotiate to acquire at actuarial value. The IRS § 7520 tables give you an objective number to work from. A 71-year-old with an 18-year life expectancy holds a life estate worth roughly $58,000 on a $235,000 property. Budget accordingly and approach the conversation professionally. Always pay for the life tenant's independent counsel — every cent of that cost buys you enforceability you cannot afford to lose. The Template 23 Life Estate Release Deed is the instrument. Get it right.
