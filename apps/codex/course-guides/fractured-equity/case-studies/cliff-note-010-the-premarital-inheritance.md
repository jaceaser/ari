## The Situation

Frederick Holloway inherited a lake house from his grandfather in 1995. He was unmarried at the time. He married Vivienne in 2003 — eight years after the inheritance. The lake house remained titled in his sole name throughout the marriage. He did not commingle community funds into it. He and Vivienne never used it as a shared residence; Vivienne lived in Houston.

Frederick died intestate in 2020. He had two adult children from a prior marriage. He and Vivienne had no children together.

The question was what the property's character — separate or community — meant for how the estate distributed.

## What the Operator Did

Texas Family Code § 3.001 is clear: property acquired before marriage is the separate property of the acquiring spouse. Property inherited during marriage is also separate property under § 3.001 regardless of when acquired. Frederick's lake house qualified as separate property on both grounds: inherited before the marriage and maintained in his sole name without commingling.

The distribution rules for intestate separate property differ from community property rules, and the difference matters enormously for the surviving spouse.

Under Texas Estates Code § 201.002 applied to separate property with a surviving spouse and descendants: the surviving spouse receives a one-third life estate. The descendants take two-thirds remainder immediately plus the remaining one-third remainder after the spouse's death.

This is the opposite outcome from § 201.003 community property with outside children, where the spouse receives zero. Here, Vivienne gets a life estate.

The operator acquired the two children's combined two-thirds remainder interest for $21,500 total — Fractured Equity pricing on interests the children were happy to monetize immediately rather than wait for Vivienne to die.

Then the operator negotiated Vivienne's life estate separately. She lived in Houston. She had never used the lake house during the marriage. She had no emotional attachment to it and no practical interest in retaining the right to occupy it. Her life estate release was negotiated at $3,500 — a significant discount from actuarial value, reflecting her lack of any real-world connection to the property.

## The Outcome

The two children's remainder interests were acquired for $21,500 total. Vivienne's life estate release was negotiated at $3,500. Full consolidation for approximately $25,000.

## The Lesson

Separate property with children outside the marriage is FAR better for the surviving spouse than community property with children outside the marriage. Under § 201.003 community property rules, the surviving spouse gets zero of the decedent's half. Under § 201.002 separate property rules, the surviving spouse gets a one-third life estate. The character of the property — community vs. separate — determines everything. Know it before you start the acquisition. And a life estate held by someone with no practical connection to the property can frequently be released for a fraction of actuarial value once the life tenant understands she is holding a right she never intended to use.
