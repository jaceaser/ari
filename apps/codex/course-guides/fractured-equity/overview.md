Most real estate investors compete for the same properties: clean title, cooperative sellers, properties that can be listed on the MLS and financed with a conventional loan. Charles "Uncle Charles" Hernandez built his career in the properties everyone else walks away from — houses stuck in probate for fifteen years, properties split among twelve heirs in five states, land with a title chain that has three gaps and two forged signatures. These properties aren't rare. Every county in America has hundreds of them. The reason nobody is working them isn't that they're difficult to find. It's that most operators don't know what to do once they find them.

The Fractured Equity Codex is the complete field manual for operators who want to compete in that space. It teaches the four types of ownership fracture — ownership, title, possession, and payment obligation — and shows you the specific legal tools that resolve each one. Partial interest acquisition, partition actions, contribution suits, affidavits of heirship, quiet title — these aren't abstract legal concepts. They are deal-making tools. You'll learn how to acquire one heir's 25% interest for a few thousand dollars, use that interest to create legal leverage over the holdouts, and force a resolution through the courts if negotiation fails.

The operators who master this system work in a market where they have virtually no competition. Normal buyers, normal wholesalers, normal investors — they can't touch these properties. The title is too messy, the ownership too complex, the path to closing too uncertain. That uncertainty is your moat. When you know how to resolve the fractures, the properties that everybody else wrote off become your most profitable acquisitions. Uncle Charles has resolved hundreds of these deals across Texas and beyond, and this codex distills that experience into a replicable system any serious operator can learn and apply.
