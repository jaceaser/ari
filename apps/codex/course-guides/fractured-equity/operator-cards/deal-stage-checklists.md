## Full Deal Stage Checklists

*Checklists only work when actually used — printed and checked off, or tracked in a project management system. Knowing a checklist exists is not the same as running it. Adapt each list to the specific deal; mark non-applicable items clearly rather than leaving them ambiguous. Initial and date each completed item to create evidence of when it was done.*

---

## F.1 Initial Qualification Checklist

Before investing significant time in a target property, complete this checklist to decide whether the deal warrants deeper diligence.

### Property Identification

- [ ] Full street address and legal description obtained
- [ ] Assessor's parcel number (APN) confirmed
- [ ] Current appraised value from county appraisal district
- [ ] Estimated market value from comparable sales
- [ ] Property type and use confirmed (residential, commercial, land, mixed)
- [ ] Physical condition assessed (drive-by minimum; full inspection if access)
- [ ] Occupancy status confirmed (vacant, owner-occupied, tenant-occupied)
- [ ] Neighborhood character and market trajectory evaluated

### Preliminary Title Picture

- [ ] Current record owner identified
- [ ] Nature of title identified — individual, co-tenancy, estate, trust, LLC, other
- [ ] Chain of title traced back to at least the last clean conveyance
- [ ] Recorded deeds over the past 20–40 years reviewed
- [ ] Any prior conveyances of fractional interests identified
- [ ] Probate records searched for relevant decedents
- [ ] Liens, mortgages, judgments, and other encumbrances identified

### Tax Status

- [ ] Current property tax status — paid, delinquent
- [ ] If delinquent, amount owed and years in arrears
- [ ] Tax foreclosure calendar checked if delinquent
- [ ] Tax sale history reviewed
- [ ] Pending tax protest or lawsuit identified if any

### Strategic Qualification

- [ ] Identified which fractured-equity strategy or strategies apply to this property
- [ ] Preliminary people-picture — who needs to be located, their likely cooperation posture
- [ ] Order-of-magnitude acquisition cost estimated
- [ ] Order-of-magnitude cleanup cost estimated
- [ ] Order-of-magnitude exit value estimated
- [ ] Preliminary deal economics — does this pencil even on rough numbers
- [ ] Operational capacity check — does this fit your current pipeline capacity
- [ ] Decision to advance to deep diligence — or not

## F.2 Deep Diligence Checklist

Once a target qualifies for serious investment of time and capital, complete the deep diligence checklist before making substantive offers.

### Full Title Work

- [ ] Title commitment or preliminary title report ordered from title company
- [ ] Complete chain of title assembled and reviewed
- [ ] All deeds, mortgages, leases, easements, and recorded documents collected
- [ ] Any probate proceedings in the chain fully reviewed
- [ ] Any prior lawsuits involving the property identified and reviewed
- [ ] Any Chapter 202 heirship determinations identified and reviewed
- [ ] Any affidavits of heirship in the chain identified and reviewed
- [ ] Title company's initial view of insurability obtained

### People Identification and Location

- [ ] All known heirs, co-tenants, beneficiaries, members identified by full name
- [ ] Death certificates obtained for all relevant deceased persons
- [ ] Current contact information obtained for all living parties
- [ ] Chapter 30 breadcrumbing complete for any missing persons
- [ ] Diligent search affidavit drafted for any unlocatable persons
- [ ] Heirship analysis complete — who inherits what fractional interest
- [ ] Any representative-capacity persons identified (guardians, executors, trustees, attorneys-in-fact)
- [ ] Documents establishing representative authority reviewed

### Property Physical and Financial

- [ ] Property physically inspected (interior and exterior if possible)
- [ ] Rehab budget prepared if rehab required
- [ ] Comparable sales analysis complete for acquisition price justification
- [ ] Comparable sales analysis complete for exit value projection
- [ ] Property tax history reviewed
- [ ] HOA dues, liens, and covenants reviewed
- [ ] Environmental review if property type warrants
- [ ] Survey ordered if property boundaries or encroachments in question
- [ ] Insurance quotes obtained for the property

### Entity and Trust Diligence

- [ ] If LLC involved — full Secretary of State history, Comptroller status, operating agreement obtained, all members identified
- [ ] If trust involved — trust instrument located, all trustees and beneficiaries identified, trust administration history reviewed
- [ ] Any judicial proceedings involving the entity or trust reviewed
- [ ] Bankruptcy searches on all relevant parties complete
- [ ] Litigation searches on all relevant parties complete

### Economic Picture

- [ ] Full budget prepared — acquisition price, cleanup costs, closing costs, holding costs, rehab costs, exit costs
- [ ] Return projections calculated for each planned exit strategy
- [ ] Sensitivity analysis — what if cleanup costs run 50% over, what if exit value is 15% below plan
- [ ] Capital source and staging identified
- [ ] Go/no-go decision documented with reasoning

## F.3 Negotiation and Contract Checklist

### Pre-Contract Coordination

- [ ] Attorney engaged and briefed on the specific deal
- [ ] CPA engaged for tax-related matters
- [ ] Title company engaged and on notice
- [ ] All counterparties identified and contacted
- [ ] Counterparties' attorneys identified if represented
- [ ] Target purchase price approved and deal economics confirmed

### Term Sheet

- [ ] Term sheet drafted covering price, structure, conditions, timing
- [ ] Term sheet reviewed by attorney
- [ ] Term sheet circulated to counterparties for preliminary agreement
- [ ] Any counter-terms addressed and resolved
- [ ] Verbal agreement on principal terms documented in a confirmation letter

### Purchase Agreement

- [ ] Purchase agreement drafted by attorney based on final terms
- [ ] Representations and warranties appropriate to the transaction structure included
- [ ] Conditions precedent clearly stated
- [ ] Contingency language addressing foreseeable issues (title problems, capacity questions, franchise tax balance surprises, etc.)
- [ ] Indemnification provisions appropriate to residual risk
- [ ] Default and remedy provisions
- [ ] Timing milestones — target closing, outside closing, termination rights
- [ ] Review by operator, review by counsel, review by counterparty's counsel
- [ ] Final execution by all parties

### Earnest Money and Escrow

- [ ] Earnest money amount and terms agreed
- [ ] Escrow agent confirmed
- [ ] Earnest money deposited per contract
- [ ] Receipt of earnest money confirmed

## F.4 Cleanup and Closing Checklist

Between contract execution and closing, the cleanup work and closing preparation happen in parallel.

### Legal Cleanup Work

- [ ] If heirship work needed — determination of heirship initiated, attorney ad litem appointed if applicable, publication if applicable, judgment obtained
- [ ] If partition proceedings needed — lawsuit filed, service on all parties, commissioner appointed if applicable, judgment obtained
- [ ] If defunct-LLC reinstatement needed — past-due reports filed, Comptroller payoff paid, tax clearance letter obtained, SoS reinstatement application filed and approved
- [ ] If trust cleanup needed — successor trustee appointment, court approvals, trust modification or termination proceedings as applicable
- [ ] If service by publication needed — diligent search affidavit filed, publication completed, citation published, ad litem appointed if applicable, judgment obtained
- [ ] If guardianship involved — court approvals obtained for the sale
- [ ] Any other specific court proceedings completed

### Title Work

- [ ] Title commitment updated to reflect cleanup work
- [ ] All required title company documentation provided — judgments, reinstatement papers, trust documents, affidavits, court orders
- [ ] Any title exceptions negotiated, removed, or endorsed around
- [ ] Survey completed if required
- [ ] Title insurance commitment approved for issuance at closing

### Documentation and Papers

- [ ] Deed drafted by attorney
- [ ] If applicable — memorandum of trust prepared
- [ ] If applicable — affidavits of authority prepared
- [ ] If applicable — membership interest assignments drafted
- [ ] If applicable — winding-up documentation prepared
- [ ] Closing statement reviewed
- [ ] All parties confirmed available for closing date
- [ ] Funds confirmed available at acquirer's source

### Closing Mechanics

- [ ] Closing date confirmed and all parties scheduled
- [ ] Closing location and method (in-person, mail-away) confirmed
- [ ] Funds wired to title company or escrow agent
- [ ] All closing documents executed
- [ ] Deeds recorded in county real property records
- [ ] Memorandum of trust recorded if applicable
- [ ] Any court orders recorded if applicable
- [ ] Title insurance policy issued
- [ ] Recording confirmations received
- [ ] Closing statement signed and filed
- [ ] Funds disbursed to all parties per closing statement

## F.5 Post-Closing Checklist

### Property Transition

- [ ] Property insurance bound in acquirer's name
- [ ] Utilities transferred or newly established
- [ ] Property tax authority notified of new owner
- [ ] Property tax address changed to acquirer's address
- [ ] HOA notified if applicable
- [ ] Any existing tenants notified of new owner
- [ ] Leases reviewed and any required transitions handled
- [ ] Locks changed and access secured
- [ ] Property inspected post-closing for any last-minute issues

### Entity Administration

- [ ] If LLC retained — entity operating going forward under new ownership, annual franchise tax obligations scheduled, registered agent current
- [ ] If LLC being terminated — certificate of termination filed with SoS, final franchise tax return filed, final IRS return filed
- [ ] If trust retained — trust administration continues under new structure
- [ ] If trust terminating — final distribution complete, final K-1s issued, trust formally terminated, final tax return filed

### Financial and Tax

- [ ] All deal costs recorded in acquirer's books
- [ ] 1099s or other tax reporting prepared for counterparties as required
- [ ] Acquirer's CPA briefed on acquisition for income tax planning
- [ ] Any tax elections considered (§ 1031, § 121, etc.)

### Documentation and File Retention

- [ ] Complete deal file assembled with all diligence materials, correspondence, contracts, court filings, closing documents, and post-closing records
- [ ] Digital scans of all documents in organized electronic file
- [ ] Backup copy maintained in separate location
- [ ] Deal file indexed for future retrieval
- [ ] Retention period confirmed (indefinite for fractured-equity deals is recommended)

### Exit Preparation

- [ ] Exit strategy confirmed (matching the exit selected during underwriting)
- [ ] If rehab exit — contractor engaged, scope finalized, budget confirmed, timeline set
- [ ] If wholetail or wholesale — buyer outreach initiated
- [ ] If buy-and-hold — property management engaged, marketing begun
- [ ] If owner financing — buyer qualification and loan documentation prepared
- [ ] If partition — court proceeding initiated
- [ ] Hold period calendar for refinance-out timing

## F.6 Professional Team Checklist

*Not per-deal — foundational. Build this team before your first deal closes, not during it.*

### Core Legal Team

- [ ] Real estate attorney with fractured-equity experience engaged
- [ ] Probate and estate attorney with heirship experience engaged
- [ ] Trust and estate attorney for trust-related matters engaged
- [ ] Business entity attorney for defunct-LLC matters engaged
- [ ] Bankruptcy attorney available for bankruptcy-adjacent cases
- [ ] Litigation attorney available for contested matters

### Tax Team

- [ ] CPA familiar with real estate acquisitions engaged
- [ ] CPA familiar with Texas franchise tax (or equivalent state tax) engaged
- [ ] Trust taxation expertise available (Form 1041 and related)

### Title and Insurance

- [ ] At least two title companies with fractured-equity experience
- [ ] Specific title underwriters identified and relationships started
- [ ] Title insurance premium and endorsement pricing understood
- [ ] Property and casualty insurance broker engaged

### Property Services

- [ ] Contractor or general contractor for rehab work
- [ ] Property management company for hold strategies
- [ ] Surveyor
- [ ] Appraiser
- [ ] Environmental professional
- [ ] Handyman and maintenance service
- [ ] Lawn/exterior maintenance

### Research and Investigation

- [ ] Professional skip-trace account (TLO, LexisNexis, or IDI) established with permissible purpose
- [ ] Consumer people-search subscriptions as needed
- [ ] Genealogy database subscriptions
- [ ] Private investigator relationship for complex cases

### Capital

- [ ] Primary capital source identified and available
- [ ] Backup capital source for unexpected needs
- [ ] Refinance lenders identified for BRRRR exits
- [ ] Owner-financing buyer pool if applicable

### Administrative Infrastructure

- [ ] Company entity formed if operating through an LLC or other structure
- [ ] Business banking established
- [ ] Project management / deal tracking system in place
- [ ] Document management and retention system
- [ ] Professional email, phone, mailing address

## F.7 Deal Documentation Checklist

*Every deal file should contain all of the following, retained indefinitely.*

### Research and Diligence

- [ ] Property records and chain of title documents
- [ ] Probate records for all relevant decedents
- [ ] Death certificates
- [ ] Marriage and divorce records as relevant
- [ ] Entity records from Secretary of State
- [ ] Franchise tax records from Comptroller
- [ ] Trust instruments and amendments
- [ ] Skip-trace reports and database searches
- [ ] Family interviews and investigation notes
- [ ] Title commitments and updates
- [ ] Appraisal reports and comparable sales
- [ ] Physical inspection records and photographs

### Correspondence

- [ ] All written correspondence with counterparties
- [ ] Logs of phone calls and verbal conversations
- [ ] Correspondence with attorneys, CPAs, and other professionals
- [ ] Correspondence with title companies
- [ ] Correspondence with courts and government agencies

### Legal Filings and Proceedings

- [ ] All pleadings filed in any court proceeding
- [ ] All court orders and judgments
- [ ] Diligent search affidavits
- [ ] Publication tear sheets and proofs of publication
- [ ] Ad litem reports and recommendations
- [ ] Affidavits of heirship
- [ ] Reinstatement applications and approvals

### Transaction Documents

- [ ] Purchase agreement and all amendments
- [ ] Earnest money documents
- [ ] All assignments, consents, and authorizations
- [ ] Deeds and other recorded instruments
- [ ] Memoranda of trust
- [ ] Closing statement
- [ ] Funding records and wire confirmations
- [ ] Title insurance policy and endorsements

### Post-Closing

- [ ] Property transition records
- [ ] Entity and trust administration records if applicable
- [ ] Tax reporting and filings
- [ ] Exit strategy documentation

## F.8 Red Flag Checklist

*Run during diligence. Any yes triggers deeper investigation or a decision to walk away.*

- [ ] Counterparty cannot or will not identify themselves with standard identification
- [ ] Counterparty pressures for fast closing beyond what makes sense
- [ ] Counterparty refuses to engage with counsel or discourages you from doing so
- [ ] Funds flowing from unexplained sources
- [ ] Property appraisal or value claims that do not match comparables
- [ ] Rehab budget estimates that seem unrealistically low
- [ ] Title issues that the counterparty dismisses rather than addresses
- [ ] Chain of title with suspicious or unexplained gaps
- [ ] Recent transfers that appear designed to defeat creditor claims
- [ ] Counterparty with pending litigation, recent bankruptcy, or known fraud history
- [ ] Property with recent insurance claims that do not match the physical condition
- [ ] Environmental concerns that are minimized but not documented as resolved
- [ ] HOA disputes or covenant violations in active litigation
- [ ] Pending tax foreclosure that is closer than counterparty acknowledges
- [ ] Family members with conflicting accounts of who inherited what
- [ ] Missing or unaccounted-for heirs whose absence cannot be diligently explained
- [ ] Prior judgments or orders that are ambiguous or potentially defective
- [ ] Documents that appear forged, altered, or irregular
- [ ] Any pattern of concealment or reluctance to share relevant information

## Using the Checklists in Practice

Checklists only work when actually used — printed and checked off, or tracked in a project management system. Knowing a checklist exists is not the same as running it. Adapt each list to the specific deal; mark non-applicable items clearly rather than leaving them ambiguous.

Initial and date each completed item to create evidence of when it was done, and retain the completed checklists with the deal file as part of the diligence documentation. The checklists should evolve with your practice — add items that prove important in your market, remove ones that don't.

A completed checklist is a record of steps taken, not a substitute for judgment. The operator who runs through a list without thinking about what the results mean is no better off than the operator who skipped it.
