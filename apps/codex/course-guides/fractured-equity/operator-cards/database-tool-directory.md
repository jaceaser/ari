## Full Database and Tool Directory

*The tool landscape changes. Verify availability and pricing before relying on any tool described here. This is a current reference, not a permanent list.*

---

## G.1 Property Records and Real Estate Data

### County-Level Property Records

County appraisal districts, recorders, and clerks are the primary sources for property records. Every county publishes its own records, typically through a county website. For volume work, know the specific interfaces and data available in your target counties.

**County Appraisal District Websites** — Each Texas county maintains a Central Appraisal District (CAD) website with property ownership, appraised value, parcel maps, and tax history. Free access. Key examples: HCAD (Harris County), DCAD (Dallas County), Travis CAD.

**County Clerk Websites** — County clerks maintain deed and real property records. Access varies — some counties offer free online search, others charge subscription fees, others require in-person visits.

**District Clerk Websites** — District clerks maintain court records including partition suits, quiet title actions, and related litigation. Access varies by county.

**County Tax Assessor-Collector Websites** — Tax status, delinquency, and tax sale information. Some counties route tax foreclosure matters through specific outside law firms (Linebarger, Perdue Brandon, McCreary Veselka Bragg & Allen) whose websites are themselves primary data sources for upcoming tax sales.

### National Property Data Platforms

**PropStream** — National property data platform with ownership, mortgage, equity estimates, and lead-filtering tools. Subscription starts around $99/month. Strong for building acquisition lists.

**PropertyRadar** — Property data and skip-trace platform, especially strong in California and western states. Subscription-based. Good for identifying distressed properties and owner contact information.

**Reonomy** — Commercial property data platform — ownership, portfolios, and contact information for commercial and multi-family property. Higher-priced subscription.

**ATTOM Data Solutions** — Enterprise-scale property data aggregator. API-based access. Used for programmatic research.

**REIPro** — Real estate investor platform with list-building, skip-trace, and CRM features. Subscription starts around $99/month.

**ListSource** — CoreLogic's property list-building platform. Subscription-based. Strong for targeted marketing lists.

**DataTree by First American** — Title and property data platform. Requires professional account setup.

### MLS and Comparable Sales

**Local MLS Access** — Multiple Listing Service access typically requires being a licensed agent or having a relationship with one. Provides the highest-quality comparable sales and market data.

**Zillow/Redfin/Realtor.com** — Consumer-facing property portals with estimated values, recent sales, and listing history. Free access. Useful for quick comparable checks but estimates can be inaccurate for unique or distressed properties.

## G.2 People Search and Skip Trace

### Free and Consumer People-Search Sites

Consumer people-search sites pull from public records and marketing databases. They are free or low-cost and often produce workable starting points. Coverage varies — a person may appear on one site with a current address and not appear on another at all.

**TruePeopleSearch** — Free people-search site. Often has current addresses, phone numbers, and relatives. Start here for preliminary searches.

**FastPeopleSearch** — Similar to TruePeopleSearch. Free. Good for cross-referencing.

**ThatsThem** — Free people-search with address and phone information. Another cross-reference source.

**Whitepages** — Classic people-search site. Basic searches free; premium features subscription-based.

**Spokeo** — Consumer people-search with social media cross-references. Subscription-based.

**BeenVerified** — Consumer background check service. Subscription-based. Provides addresses, phones, relatives, and more comprehensive reports.

**Intelius** — Similar to BeenVerified. Subscription-based.

### Professional Skip-Trace Databases

Professional databases require permissible-purpose justification and are significantly more expensive, but they provide faster, deeper, and more current information than consumer sites. Appropriate for operators doing volume work with legitimate property research purposes.

**TLO (TransUnion TLOxp)** — One of the most comprehensive professional skip-trace platforms. Addresses, phones, associates, property, vehicles, and more. Requires permissible-purpose account. Per-search pricing; typical operator subscription runs $150–400/month plus per-search fees.

**LexisNexis Accurint** — Professional investigative database widely used by attorneys, title companies, and investigators. Deep data including addresses, business affiliations, court records, and related individuals. Permissible purpose required. Higher price point.

**IDI Core** — Professional skip-trace platform used by investigators and collectors. Similar capabilities to TLO. Permissible purpose required.

**Thomson Reuters CLEAR** — Enterprise-level investigative platform. Comprehensive but expensive. Used by larger firms.

### Social Media

**Facebook** — Still the most valuable single social media platform for finding people, especially older and rural populations. Search by name, location, and relatives. Free.

**LinkedIn** — Best for locating professionals and business contacts. Free basic access; paid subscriptions for deeper search capabilities.

**Instagram** — Useful for younger targets and for confirming identity through photos. Free.

**Twitter/X** — Less useful for standard people-search but can confirm identity and current interests.

**TikTok** — Increasingly useful for locating younger people and confirming current activity.

## G.3 Genealogy and Family History

### Primary Genealogy Platforms

**Ancestry.com** — The largest consumer genealogy platform. Access to census records, vital records, immigration records, military records, and user-submitted family trees. Subscription-based, typically $25–50/month. Essential for complex family-tree work.

**FamilySearch.org** — Free genealogy platform operated by the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. Massive collection of vital records and family trees. Completely free. Excellent complement to Ancestry.

**MyHeritage** — International genealogy platform with strong coverage of European records. Subscription-based.

**Newspapers.com** — Historical newspaper archive — obituaries, marriage announcements, local news. Subscription-based. Invaluable for finding obituaries and family history details.

**Newspaperarchive.com** — Alternative historical newspaper archive.

**GenealogyBank** — Another historical newspaper archive focused on genealogy.

### Cemetery and Death Records

**FindAGrave** — Free database of cemetery records and grave photos. Often includes biographical information and family links. Essential for confirming deaths and identifying burial locations.

**BillionGraves** — Alternative cemetery database with GPS-tagged headstone photos. Free.

**Social Security Death Index (SSDI)** — Available through Ancestry and FamilySearch. Identifies deaths from Social Security records. Useful for confirming whether someone is deceased.

**Legacy.com** — Consolidated obituary database. Free search; some newspapers limit access without subscription.

### Government Vital Records

Every state maintains vital records — birth, death, marriage, divorce. Access varies by state.

**Texas Department of State Health Services** — Texas vital records. Death certificates available through VitalChek or directly. Fees apply.

**VitalChek** — National service for ordering certified copies of vital records from most states. Fees vary by state and record type.

**State-specific vital records offices** — Most states have online ordering for death certificates, birth certificates, and marriage certificates. Check the state health department or equivalent.

## G.4 Entity Research

### Texas-Specific

**Texas Secretary of State Business Filings Search (SOSDirect)** — Primary source for Texas entity records — formation documents, filing history, registered agents, officers. Per-search fees apply for detailed documents. Free basic search.

**Texas Comptroller Franchise Tax Account Status** — Check an entity's franchise tax status — in good standing, forfeited, or other. Free public search at the Comptroller's website. Essential for defunct-LLC qualification.

**Texas State Bar Attorney Directory** — For identifying the drafting attorney of a trust, the registered agent attorney, or opposing counsel.

### Other States

**State Secretary of State Websites** — Every state has an equivalent to Texas SOSDirect. Names vary — California Secretary of State Business Search, Florida Division of Corporations Sunbiz, Delaware Division of Corporations, etc.

**OpenCorporates** — Aggregated database of corporate records from many jurisdictions globally. Free basic search. Useful for tracking entity relationships across state lines.

### Federal

**FinCEN Beneficial Ownership Reporting Database** — Federal database of entity beneficial ownership information under the Corporate Transparency Act. Access restricted to law enforcement and certain authorized users.

**SEC EDGAR** — Securities filings for publicly traded companies. Useful when a counterparty or related entity is a reporting company.

## G.5 Court Records and Legal Research

### Texas State Courts

**Texas Judiciary Public Access** — Portal to Texas state court records. Varies by county — some counties publish online, others require in-person visits.

**County District Clerk Websites** — Local court records including civil suits, partition actions, probate matters. Access varies widely.

### Federal Courts

**PACER (Public Access to Court Electronic Records)** — Federal court records — district courts, bankruptcy courts, appellate courts. Per-page charges after the first $30 per quarter. Essential for bankruptcy searches.

**CourtListener** — Free alternative access to federal court records for many cases.

### Legal Research Platforms

**Westlaw** — Premier legal research platform. Subscription. Used primarily by attorneys.

**LexisNexis** — Alternative legal research platform.

**Casetext** — Newer legal research platform with AI search features. Generally lower-priced than Westlaw or Lexis.

**Google Scholar Case Law** — Free case law search. Limited but workable for basic research.

## G.6 Specialized Search Resources

### Criminal and Corrections

**Texas Department of Criminal Justice Inmate Locator** — Free search for current Texas state prison inmates.

**Federal Bureau of Prisons Inmate Locator** — Free search for current federal inmates.

**State Inmate Locators (various states)** — Every state has an inmate locator for its state prisons. Links collected through sites like VineLink.

**County Jail Rosters** — Many counties publish current jail rosters online. Useful for locating people recently arrested or held pre-trial.

**VineLink** — National service for tracking incarcerated persons and victim notifications.

### Care Facilities and Medical

**Medicare.gov Nursing Home Compare** — Federal database of nursing homes including ratings, staffing, and ownership.

**State Long-Term Care Facility Databases** — Every state has a database of licensed long-term care facilities. Texas uses the Texas Health and Human Services Long-term Care Provider Search.

**Social Security Administration** — For confirming deaths; death benefits processing often produces death confirmations before other public databases reflect them.

### Military

**National Personnel Records Center** — Federal repository of military service records. Next-of-kin can request; others may request under FOIA or with the veteran's consent.

**VA Death Records** — Veterans Affairs maintains deceased veteran records. Useful for locating deceased veterans and their burial sites.

**Military Service Record Requests** — SF-180 form to request military service records; available through NARA.

**Department of Veterans Affairs Benefits Tracking** — For living veterans, VA records can sometimes help establish current location through benefits addresses.

### International

**State Department Special Consular Services** — Can sometimes assist in locating U.S. citizens abroad in specific circumstances.

**Consulates and Embassies** — Country-specific consulates can sometimes assist with locating citizens.

**Hometown Associations** — For Mexican and Central American migrants, hometown associations (clubes de oriundos) can be valuable community contacts.

**Mexico's Registro Civil** — State-level civil registries in Mexico maintain birth, marriage, and death records. Access varies by Mexican state.

**Catholic Church Parish Records** — For Catholic families, parish records often contain baptism, marriage, and death records. Write directly to parishes of interest.

### Religious Records

**Archdiocese and Diocesan Archives** — For Catholic records, archdioceses maintain central archives. Useful when a specific parish cannot be identified.

**Denominational Archives** — Protestant denominations, Jewish communities, Orthodox churches each maintain records that vary by tradition and region.

## G.8 Free and Open Resources

A significant portion of fractured-equity research can be done with free resources. New operators working on early deals should lean heavily on free resources and add paid tools only as volume justifies the subscription costs.

**Google** — Simple web search often produces surprising results. Search the target's full name in quotes, combined with location, occupation, or other identifiers.

**Google Maps and Street View** — Visual inspection of properties from anywhere. Essential for preliminary qualification of target properties.

**Internet Archive (archive.org)** — Historical website snapshots, sometimes useful for finding discontinued company pages or old news articles.

**LinkedIn Basic Search** — Free LinkedIn searches can produce current employer and location for many professionals.

**Facebook Basic Search** — Free Facebook searches — often the single most productive free people-search tool.

## G.9 State-Specific Variations

Operators working in states other than Texas should identify the state equivalents for each tool category:

1. Secretary of State business filings — every state has one
2. State tax authority for entity tax status — California Franchise Tax Board, Delaware Division of Corporations franchise tax, etc.
3. State court records portals — vary widely by state, from excellent to nearly nonexistent
4. County property records — every county in every state has its own
5. Vital records authorities — every state has a health department or equivalent
6. State inmate locators — every state has one for its state prison system
7. State-specific genealogy and historical societies — many states have excellent free resources

Before starting work in a new state, spend a day mapping the state's free and paid research resources. The quality of state-level public records varies enormously — some states offer clean, searchable, comprehensive online access; others require in-person courthouse visits. Know what you're working with before committing to a market.

## G.10 Permissible Purpose and Legal Compliance

Several professional tools require permissible-purpose certification under federal privacy laws.

**Federal statutes affecting access:**
- Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (15 U.S.C. § 6801 et seq.) — limits access to certain financial information
- Driver's Privacy Protection Act (18 U.S.C. § 2721 et seq.) — limits access to motor vehicle records
- Fair Credit Reporting Act — limits use of consumer reports for certain purposes
- Various state privacy laws — particularly California's CCPA and similar laws in other states

**Typical permissible purposes for real estate work:**
- Investigation in connection with a real estate transaction or property acquisition
- Due diligence for a business transaction
- Legal process, including litigation support
- Locating a party for whom legitimate business needs require contact

**Compliance practices:**
- Complete the tool provider's permissible purpose onboarding honestly
- Maintain records of why each search was conducted
- Do not share or resell data obtained through these tools
- Do not use the tools for personal curiosity or for purposes outside the permitted scope
- Consult counsel when a specific use is unclear

**Consequences of misuse:** Federal civil penalties, federal criminal charges in serious cases, loss of access to the tool (and sometimes to all tools sharing common underwriting), and civil liability to the subjects of misused searches. Operators have been prosecuted for misusing these tools. Compliance is not optional.

## G.11 Managing Tool Costs

**Start free, upgrade as volume justifies.** Most first-year operators can complete most work with free tools plus occasional per-search payments at paid services. Reserve monthly subscriptions for when deal volume justifies the fixed cost.

**Budget tool costs into deal economics.** Include professional research costs in the cost side of each deal. A $400 skip-trace report for a $50,000 profit deal is negligible; a $400 skip-trace report on a $3,000 wholesale fee deal is significant. Match tool spending to deal economics.

**Track what you're paying for what results.** Some tools are more productive than others in your specific market and deal type. Track your usage and cancel subscriptions that are not paying for themselves.

**Use firm or attorney relationships for occasional access.** Your real estate attorney likely has Westlaw or Lexis access; your title company may have DataTree or similar. One-off queries can sometimes be run through these professional relationships.

**Stay current.** The tool landscape evolves. Follow real estate investor communities (BiggerPockets, local meetups), subscribe to industry newsletters, attend conferences, maintain relationships with other operators willing to share what is working for them, and revisit your own tool stack annually.
