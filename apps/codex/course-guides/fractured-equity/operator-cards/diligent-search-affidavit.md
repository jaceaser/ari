## Diligent Search Affidavit — Full Template

*Template for Texas; adapt with counsel for other states. Every state requires some form of diligent search affidavit before authorizing service by publication. The Mullane v. Central Hanover Bank & Trust Co. notice standard applies nationally.*

---

**AFFIDAVIT OF DILIGENT SEARCH**

STATE OF TEXAS                          §
                                        §
COUNTY OF [COUNTY]                      §

BEFORE ME, the undersigned authority, on this day personally appeared [Affiant's Full Name], who is personally known to me (or who was proved to me through identifying documents described below) to be the person whose name is subscribed to this Affidavit, and who, after being duly sworn, upon oath deposed and stated as follows:

### 1. IDENTIFICATION OF AFFIANT

1.1 My name is [Affiant's Full Name]. I am over the age of 18, of sound mind, and fully competent to make this Affidavit. My residence address is [Address]. I make this Affidavit based on my personal knowledge of the facts stated herein.

1.2 I am [describe role — e.g., "the applicant in the above-referenced matter"; "the attorney of record for the applicant"; "an investigator retained by the applicant"]. In that role, I conducted or supervised the search for the missing person described below.

### 2. THE PERSON BEING SOUGHT

2.1 This Affidavit concerns the search for [Missing Person's Full Name] ("the Missing Person"). [State all known identifying information]:

- Full name, including any known aliases: [Names]
- Date of birth, if known: [Date]
- Last known address: [Address and approximate time period at that address]
- Relationship to the matter: [e.g., "named heir of (Decedent's name), a deceased owner of the real property at (address)"]
- Reason the Missing Person must be located: [e.g., "to determine heirship in the underlying probate proceeding"; "to provide service of process in the underlying partition action"; "to obtain the Missing Person's signature on documents necessary to clear title"]

### 3. SEARCH PERIOD

3.1 The search activities described in this Affidavit were conducted during the period from [Start Date] through [End Date]. Over this period, I (or those working under my supervision) committed approximately [number] hours to locating the Missing Person.

### 4. SOCIAL BREADCRUMBING WORK

4.1 I conducted the following interviews and outreach to family members, friends, neighbors, and other persons who might have information about the Missing Person:

[Interview 1] On [Date], I [interviewed/attempted to interview] [Name of Person], who is [relationship — e.g., "the Missing Person's sister"]. [State contact method — phone, in-person, email.] [Summarize what the interview produced — what the person knew, what the person did not know, any leads or dead ends.] **Example:** "On March 15, 2024, I interviewed Jane Smith, the Missing Person's sister, by phone at (555) 123-4567. Ms. Smith stated that she had last heard from her brother in approximately 2015, at which time he was living in Austin, Texas. She did not know where he had moved after that and had no current contact information. She provided names of two additional family members who might know more: John Smith (their cousin) and Mary Johnson (a family friend)."

[Interview 2] [Same format]

[Continue for each interview]

4.2 I conducted the following searches of publicly available obituaries and death records, to determine whether the Missing Person is deceased:

[Obituary search 1] On [Date], I searched [database — e.g., Legacy.com, Newspapers.com, FindAGrave, local newspaper archives] for [the Missing Person's name] and any variations thereof. [State results — e.g., "No obituary was found."]

[Continue for each database searched]

4.3 I conducted the following social media searches:

[Social media search 1] On [Date], I searched [platform — e.g., Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram] for [the Missing Person's name]. [State results — e.g., "I located a Facebook profile that appeared to match the Missing Person based on age, location, and photos. I sent a message to this profile on (date). As of the date of this Affidavit, I have received no response."]

[Continue for each platform]

4.4 I visited or caused to be visited the following last-known addresses and spoke with current occupants or neighbors:

[Address 1] On [Date], I [visited / had a process server visit] the address at [address]. [State what was found — current occupant, prior occupant, neighbor interviews, any leads.]

[Continue for each address investigated]

4.5 I contacted the following organizations, community groups, or institutions:

[Organization 1] On [Date], I contacted [organization — e.g., the Masonic Lodge of (city); the (church name) parish office; the hometown association for (pueblo name)]. I inquired whether they had records of the Missing Person or current contact information. [State results.]

[Continue for each organization contacted]

### 5. ADVANCED SKIP TRACING WORK — FOUNDATIONAL LAYER

5.1 I conducted the following searches of public records:

- Texas Secretary of State business records for any business affiliations [date, result]
- County real property records in [counties] for any property ownership [date, result]
- Voter registration records in [counties or states] [date, result]
- Court records at the county, state, and federal level in [jurisdictions] for any litigation involving the Missing Person [date, result]
- Tax appraisal records [date, result]
- Marriage and divorce records [date, result]
- Professional license records in applicable areas [date, result]

5.2 I conducted the following searches of consumer people-search databases:

- TruePeopleSearch on [date] — [result]
- FastPeopleSearch on [date] — [result]
- ThatsThem on [date] — [result]
- BeenVerified on [date] — [result]
- Spokeo on [date] — [result]
- Intelius on [date] — [result]
- Whitepages on [date] — [result]
- [Other databases searched, if any]

5.3 I conducted the following searches of professional skip-trace databases (where I have legitimate permissible purpose):

- TLO (TransUnion TLOxp) on [date] — [result, including whether any current address, phones, associates, or other contact information was located]
- LexisNexis Accurint on [date] — [result]
- IDI on [date] — [result]
- [Other professional databases, if used]

5.4 I consulted the following genealogy databases:

- Ancestry.com on [date] — [result]
- FamilySearch.org on [date] — [result]
- MyHeritage on [date] — [result]
- FindAGrave on [date] — [result]
- Newspapers.com on [date] — [result]

### 6. SPECIALIZED SEARCH TRAILS

6.1 Based on information developed during the social breadcrumbing work, I pursued the following specialized search trails:

[Trail 1 — Criminal, if triggered] Information from [source of intel] indicated that the Missing Person [describe the triggering information — e.g., "had been incarcerated at some point"]. I therefore searched [resources — e.g., Texas Department of Criminal Justice inmate locator; Federal Bureau of Prisons inmate locator; county jail rosters] on [dates]. [Results.]

[Trail 2 — Care Facility, if triggered] Information from [source] indicated the Missing Person [describe intel — e.g., "may be a resident in a nursing home in Oklahoma"]. I searched [resources — e.g., Medicare.gov Nursing Home Compare; state long-term care facility databases] on [dates]. [Results.] I sent letters to the social work departments of [number] facilities in [area] on [dates]. [Describe responses received.]

[Trail 3 — International, if triggered] Information from [source] indicated the Missing Person [describe intel — e.g., "had moved to Mexico"]. I [describe search efforts — e.g., "contacted the hometown association for (pueblo) on (date) and spoke with (name), who provided contact information for a cousin of the Missing Person residing in (city, state, Mexico)"]. [Describe results.]

[Trail 4 — Catholic Church, if triggered] Information from [source] indicated that the Missing Person's family is Catholic and has ties to [parish/region]. I wrote to [parish] on [date] requesting information about [specific records — baptism, marriage, death]. [Describe response received.]

[Trail 5 — Military, if triggered] Information from [source] indicated that the Missing Person served in the [branch of service]. I [describe search efforts — e.g., "contacted the National Personnel Records Center; searched DD-214 filings in (county); contacted (veterans organization)"]. [Results.]

6.2 [If any specialized trails were not triggered by the intel] I did not pursue searches in the following specialized areas because the information developed during the social breadcrumbing work did not indicate those areas were applicable to the Missing Person: [list any areas not pursued and why].

### 7. DIRECT OUTREACH ATTEMPTS

7.1 I attempted the following direct contact with the Missing Person:

[Letter 1] On [Date], I sent a letter by [certified mail / regular first-class mail] to [address]. The letter [briefly describe contents — e.g., "identified the applicant, explained the property matter, and asked the Missing Person to contact me if he received the letter"]. [Result — e.g., "The letter was returned on (date) as undeliverable, marked 'No Such Address.'" Or: "The letter was not returned but no response has been received."]

[Letter 2] [Same format]

[Phone call 1] On [Date], I attempted to call [phone number obtained from (source)]. [Result — e.g., "The number was disconnected." Or: "I left a voicemail message. As of the date of this Affidavit, I have received no return call."]

[Email 1] On [Date], I sent an email to [email address obtained from (source)]. [Result.]

[Continue for each direct contact attempt]

### 8. RESULTS OF THE SEARCH

8.1 Despite the foregoing efforts, I have been unable to locate the Missing Person. Specifically:

- No current address for the Missing Person has been confirmed
- No current phone number for the Missing Person has been confirmed
- No current email address for the Missing Person has been confirmed
- No person interviewed has been able to provide current contact information for the Missing Person
- No database search has produced current contact information for the Missing Person
- No direct outreach has produced a response from the Missing Person
- [Describe any information that was obtained but that proved outdated or insufficient]

8.2 [If the search suggests the Missing Person may be deceased but has not been confirmed] Certain information developed during the search suggests the possibility that the Missing Person may be deceased — specifically, [describe — e.g., "family members reported that the Missing Person had not been in contact with anyone in the family for over twenty years, and a preliminary Social Security Death Index search on (date) revealed a possible match that would require additional verification"]. However, I have not been able to confirm the Missing Person's death through a death certificate or other authoritative record.

8.3 Based on the scope and intensity of the search described above, and based on the absence of any meaningful result despite that search, in my judgment the Missing Person cannot be located through reasonably available means. Further search effort would, in my judgment, not be reasonably likely to produce the Missing Person's current whereabouts.

### 9. SUPPORTING DOCUMENTATION

9.1 The following documents, which corroborate the statements in this Affidavit, are retained in my file and are available upon request:

- Database search reports from TLO, LexisNexis, and other professional tools dated [dates]
- Screenshots and printouts from consumer people-search sites
- Contemporaneous notes of family interviews and other phone calls
- Copies of all letters and emails sent, including records of return-as-undeliverable where applicable
- Responses received from organizations contacted
- Any other documentary evidence produced during the search

9.2 [Optional, if the court or counsel prefers] Selected key documents are attached to this Affidavit as Exhibits [list], specifically: [identify the most important documents].

### 10. DECLARATION AND OATH

10.1 I have read this Affidavit carefully, I understand its contents, and I swear under oath that all statements made in this Affidavit are true and correct to the best of my personal knowledge and belief.

10.2 I understand that this Affidavit will be relied upon by the Court, by the attorney ad litem (if one is appointed), by title companies, by other parties to the underlying proceeding, and potentially by future courts reviewing the judgment that results from the underlying proceeding.

10.3 I understand that making false statements in this Affidavit would constitute perjury under Texas law and expose me to criminal and civil liability.

_______________________________________
[Affiant's Full Name], Affiant

## Preparation Notes

**Document as you go, not at the end.** The strongest affidavits are built from contemporaneous records. Log each search, each call, each interview at the time it happens. Affidavits drafted from memory at the end of a long search are weaker than affidavits drafted from a contemporaneous log.

**Retain the underlying evidence.** Database search reports, screenshots, interview notes, letters, and email records should be retained in an organized file for at least ten years — indefinitely is better for cases where title claims could surface decades later.

**Be honest about what you didn't do.** If certain search avenues were not pursued — because the intel did not trigger them, because the cost was disproportionate, or because they were not reasonably available — say so. An affidavit that explains what was pursued and why, and acknowledges what was not pursued and why, is more credible than one that overclaims.

**Coordinate with counsel.** The operator conducts the search, but the affidavit itself should be reviewed by counsel before filing. Counsel ensures it meets the specific requirements of the court or proceeding.

**The affidavit's long-term role.** A well-crafted affidavit serves far beyond the immediate proceeding. It supports the original judgment, defends against bill-of-review challenges when a missing heir surfaces years later, supports title insurance underwriting, and builds the operator's reputation in the local legal community. Every affidavit you produce becomes part of your track record.
