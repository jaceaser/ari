checklist:
  - Confirm property owner is deceased and estate was never probated
  - Pull death certificate for the decedent
  - Research whether decedent left a will (check probate court records)
  - Identify all potential heirs using intestacy statute for the state
  - Find two disinterested witnesses who knew the decedent personally
  - Witnesses must have no financial interest in the property
  - Prepare affidavit with full legal description of the property
  - Include decedent's name, date of death, marital history, all children, whether any predeceased
  - Have witnesses sign before a notary
  - Record in the county where property is located
  - Wait statutory period before relying on it as title evidence (4 years in Texas)
commonMistakes:
  - Using interested witnesses (heirs, family members)
  - Incomplete marital history (missing a prior marriage creates unknown heirs)
  - Not including the full legal description
  - Relying on it as standalone title without title insurance
  - Not researching whether a will was probated in another county
