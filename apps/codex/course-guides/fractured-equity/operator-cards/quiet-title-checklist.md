checklist:
  - Identify all potential adverse claimants (heirs, lienholders, judgment creditors)
  - Pull full title search — every instrument back to original patent
  - Engage a title curative attorney in the property's state
  - File quiet title petition naming all known adverse claimants
  - Publish notice for unknown heirs per state statute
  - File lis pendens
  - Serve all named defendants
  - Attend any required hearings
  - Obtain final judgment declaring your title
  - Record the judgment in the county real property records
  - Order new title insurance commitment based on the judgment
commonMistakes:
  - Failing to identify and name all potential claimants
  - Inadequate publication notice for unknown heirs
  - Not recording the final judgment
  - Assuming quiet title alone gets you title insurance without re-examining the chain
