Tax foreclosure intervention is the practice of acquiring fractured-title properties before or during the county tax sale process. Every county in America publishes tax delinquency lists. Most investors look at these lists as a source of auction inventory. The fractured equity operator looks at them differently: as a map of properties where ownership has broken down.

When property taxes go delinquent for multiple years, it almost always means one of a few things: the owner is dead and heirs don't know or don't care; the ownership is split across multiple heirs who each assume someone else is handling it; or the owner has abandoned the property entirely. All three scenarios create acquisition opportunities.

## Before the Auction

The real opportunity in tax foreclosure isn't the auction — it's the period between delinquency and sale. During that window, the heirs or owners still have the legal right to sell the property. If you can reach them before the county forecloses, you can buy their interests at a price that reflects the emergency: they're about to lose everything, your offer lets them walk away with something.

The county's foreclosure lawsuit is actually a useful marketing trigger. When heirs receive lawsuit paperwork from the county, many of them become suddenly motivated to engage. Some of them didn't know the taxes weren't being paid. The lawsuit creates urgency that wasn't there before.

## After the Auction

If the property sells at the tax sale, it's not necessarily lost. Many states have redemption periods — a window after the sale during which the prior owner or their heirs can reclaim the property by paying the back taxes plus penalties. Understanding your state's redemption period is critical, both because it affects deals you're trying to close and because it creates opportunities: you can acquire interests from heirs who want to exercise the redemption right but can't afford to do it themselves.

The title complications that follow a tax sale — especially when the pre-sale ownership was fractured — are exactly the kind of problems that a quiet title action can resolve.
